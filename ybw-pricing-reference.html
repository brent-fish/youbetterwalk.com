Holiday & Peak Season Pricing

Standard Holiday Uplift

+25-50%

Apply to all services during the full week surrounding major holidays: Thanksgiving week, Christmas/NYE week, July 4th week, Memorial Day weekend, Labor Day weekend. The holiday itself is never an isolated day for scheduling purposes. Communicate before the season, not the week of.

Peak Season Window

Summer + Holidays

Memorial Day through Labor Day is high-demand for pet care. Consider a modest +10-15% seasonal rate for new clients during this window.

Recommended Holiday Structure

Flat +$15/visit

A flat fee per visit is simpler for clients than a percentage - they know the number before they book. +$15/visit for drop-ins and walks; +$25-30 for overnights (when offered). State it clearly in onboarding and the FAQ.

Holiday Booking Cutoff

2 weeks out

Set a booking cutoff for peak holidays. Late bookings carry a +$10-15 convenience fee. Incentivizes planning, protects your schedule.

Cancellation Policy

Notice Given Fee Rationale
48+ hours No charge Standard window. No impact on your schedule.
24-48 hours 50% of visit Partial compensation for blocked schedule time. Apply consistently; exceptions should be rare and documented.
Under 24 hours 100% of visit Full fee. At this point the slot is unrecoverable. Non-negotiable except in genuine emergencies.
Same-day / no-show 100% of visit Full fee. You showed up. Consider a second no-show grounds for service review.
Holiday cancellation 100% of visit Regardless of notice. Holiday slots are finite and in demand. State this clearly in advance.

Payment & Billing

Accepted Methods

Venmo, Zelle, Square

Venmo Business (zero fee, ubiquitous) and Zelle (zero fee, instant via your bank) are your primary options. Square handles card payments at 2.6% - clients who prefer card pay via Square. Three options is professional without being complicated. Skip PayPal.

Billing Cycle

Weekly or per-visit

For recurring clients, weekly billing reduces friction for both sides. For new or occasional clients, per-visit until trust is established. Never let invoices age past 14 days.

Late Payment

Pause service at 7 days

A soft pause (not a termination) with a direct message is professional and usually resolves it immediately. Document it in your client notes.

Rate Increases

30 days notice

Industry standard is 30 days written notice for any rate change. Annual increases of 5-10% are normal and expected. Clients who've been with you longest deserve personal messaging, not a blanket email.

Market Context - Columbus, OH 2026

Solo independent operators in the Columbus metro typically run 10-20% above app-based platforms (Rover/Wag equivalents) on a net basis, once platform fees are factored out. Your positioning as a non-app, personal service justifies a premium. Don't undercut to compete with platforms - that's not your market.

Upper Arlington / Grandview Heights / Marble Cliff / Worthington / Clintonville are among the higher-income neighborhoods in the Columbus metro. These clients are accustomed to paying for quality and personalization. Price for that market, not the metro average.

Free meet and greet as differentiator: Several Columbus competitors (including Fur Star Pet Care) charge $25-35 for the initial meet and greet. Yours is free. This removes friction at the top of the funnel and is a genuine competitive advantage worth stating clearly in copy and every first-contact email. Do not add a fee until you have a waitlist.

Price anchoring: Your "From $22" and "From $25" language on the site is intentionally low - it gets people in the door. The actual conversation at onboarding sets the real rate. Make sure your language there moves people toward the 30-min walk ($30) as the standard, with 20-min as the exception.

Review pricing annually - at minimum at the start of each calendar year, and whenever Columbus CPI data shows meaningful inflation. Your labor rate should not stay flat while your cost of living rises.

Bottom line: At full capacity for a solo operator (8-10 walks/day, 5 days/week), your gross revenue potential at current rates is approximately $48,000-$65,000 annually before expenses. At suggested market midpoints, that range rises to $55,000-$75,000. The investment in reputation and relationships now directly compounds that number.
